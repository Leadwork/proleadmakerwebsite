"Intent data" is one of the most over-marketed terms in B2B. Every vendor sells it. Most of it is useless on its own. A handful of signals genuinely predict purchases, and the magic is in layering them.

Here are the 12 that actually move the needle.

## Tier 1: high-precision signals

These signals on their own justify outreach. Reply rates jump 3–5x when present.

### 1. New executive hire (relevant role)

A new CMO, VP of RevOps, or Head of Sales has a 60–90 day window to replace tools. They're more open to vendor conversations in those first 90 days than they will be for the next two years.

**Source:** LinkedIn job change announcements, press releases, Crunchbase.

### 2. Funding round (within 6 months)

Cash on the balance sheet plus a board pressuring for growth equals tool purchases. Seed and Series A rounds usually trigger CRM, outbound, and analytics buys within 60 days.

**Source:** Crunchbase, PitchBook, TechCrunch, founder LinkedIn posts.

### 3. Specific hiring patterns

A SaaS company posting three SDR roles is scaling outbound. A fintech posting two compliance roles is preparing for SOC 2. The roles people are hiring for telegraph the projects they're investing in.

**Source:** LinkedIn Jobs, Indeed, company careers pages.

### 4. Tech stack changes (additions or removals)

Adding HubSpot? They probably want enrichment, sales tools, and outbound. Removing Marketo? They're shopping for a replacement. The technographic signal is the most underused intent data in B2B.

**Source:** BuiltWith, Wappalyzer, Clay, manual job-posting language analysis.

## Tier 2: high-precision when layered

These signals are useful when combined with another tier-1 or tier-2 signal. Alone, they're noise.

### 5. Office expansion or geographic move

New office, new state, new country. Indicates growth, but doesn't say what they need until paired with hiring data.

### 6. Product launch or major announcement

A new product launch often triggers GTM tool re-evaluation. Pair with funding or exec hire data for higher precision.

### 7. Conference attendance / event signups

Especially industry-specific events. A team that just sent five people to SaaStr is in active learning mode about SaaS GTM.

### 8. Job postings with vendor-specific language

"Experience with Salesforce" in a posting is a tech-stack signal. "Replacing Salesforce" or "migrating from Salesforce" is gold.

## Tier 3: directional signals

Useful for scoring, not for sole-trigger outreach.

### 9. Headcount growth rate

A company growing >20% headcount YoY is in active tool-buying mode. <5% growth and they're optimizing what they have.

### 10. Website redesigns or rebrands

Indicates strategic GTM investment. Often paired with marketing tool buying.

### 11. Social media activity spike

A sudden uptick in CEO or CMO LinkedIn posting often correlates with a fundraise or product launch lead-up.

### 12. M&A activity

Acquirer or acquired? Both create 6–12 month windows of tool consolidation and re-evaluation.

## What's NOT a useful signal

Despite vendor marketing, these are noise more often than signal:

- **Generic web visit data** ("they visited your pricing page!"). Without IP-to-company resolution and a downstream action, it's a hint, not intent.
- **Topic surge data** from generic intent platforms. Useful for category awareness, not for individual outreach triggers.
- **Email engagement on someone else's campaign.** They opened your competitor's email. So did 50,000 others.

## How to layer signals

The best signal stack is 2–3 layers, no more.

**Example layered query:**

> "B2B SaaS companies, 50–500 employees, that raised Series A or B in the last 9 months, AND posted at least 1 SDR or Account Executive role in the last 30 days."

That query typically returns 200–600 companies. Every one of them has a strong reason to take a meeting. Outreach against that list converts at 8–14% — vs. 1–2% on a generic SaaS list of 20,000.

## Scoring signals

When you have signal data on every contact, build a simple weighted score:

| Signal                          | Weight |
|---------------------------------|--------|
| New exec hire (90 days)         | 30     |
| Funding round (180 days)        | 25     |
| Relevant role hiring (active)   | 20     |
| Tech stack match                | 15     |
| Office expansion                | 10     |
| Other tier-2 signals            | 5 each |

Anything scoring 50+ is a top-priority outbound target. 25–50 is medium-priority. Under 25 is nurture-only.
