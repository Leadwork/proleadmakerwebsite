Every lead gen team thinks they're failing because their messaging isn't sharp enough or their offer isn't strong enough. Usually the real problem is mechanical: small mistakes compounding into a pipeline gap.

Here are the ten most expensive mistakes we see — and exactly how to fix each one.

## 1. Sending cold email from your primary domain

You spent five years building reputation on `yourcompany.com`. Don't burn it sending 200 cold emails a day.

**Fix:** Buy a secondary sending domain (`getyourcompany.com`, `try-yourcompany.com`), set up SPF/DKIM/DMARC, and route all outbound through it.

## 2. Skipping email verification

A 10% bounce rate on a 5,000-email send is a domain-killing event. We see teams do this every week.

**Fix:** Triple-verify every email through three providers before any send. Re-verify lists older than 60 days.

## 3. Buying lists by volume, not quality

A 50,000-row list that cost $300 isn't a bargain. It's $300 plus the cost of a destroyed sending domain.

**Fix:** Buy by quality, not size. 600 signal-scored, triple-verified contacts beats 50,000 unverified rows every time.

## 4. One-channel outreach

Email alone caps at 2–4% reply rate. LinkedIn alone caps at 6–10%. Mixed cadences hit 15–25%.

**Fix:** Build a 3-channel cadence (email + LinkedIn + phone). 8 touches across 18 days.

## 5. Pitching in the first message

The hardest-converting email is the one that pitches in line 1. The highest-converting opens with a specific observation about the prospect.

**Fix:** Spend your first 30 words on what you noticed, not what you sell.

## 6. No personalization beyond the first email

Follow-ups that say "just checking in" get ignored. Follow-ups that add new context ("saw your team's announcement on Tuesday") get replies.

**Fix:** Every follow-up adds a new signal or observation. If you can't write one, drop the prospect from the cadence.

## 7. Not tracking the right metrics

Most teams track sends and open rates. Useless. The real metric is *meetings booked per 100 contacts worked*.

**Fix:** Build a dashboard with reply rate, positive reply rate, meetings booked, and meetings held — per cadence variant. Cut what doesn't work after 90 days.

## 8. Mixing personas in one sequence

A CFO and a Head of RevOps don't share pain points. Sending both the same sequence dilutes both.

**Fix:** One sequence per persona. Yes, that's more work. It's also why teams that do it convert 3x better.

## 9. No SDR → AE handoff discipline

Booked meetings no-show because of poor handoff hygiene. The number one driver of pipeline leak.

**Fix:** AE sends a personal note within 4 hours of booking, confirmation 24 hours before, SMS 30 minutes before for high-value prospects.

## 10. Treating list quality as a one-time event

A "fresh" list six months ago is now a 70%-deliverable list. Send to it and you wreck your domain.

**Fix:** Re-verify any list before re-sending. Build a cadence calendar that includes re-verification windows.

## The compound math

Each of these mistakes alone might cost you 5–10% of pipeline. Stack three of them and you're operating at 50% of potential. Most teams stack five.

The good news: every one of these fixes is mechanical, not creative. You don't need a better positioning workshop. You need a better operating discipline.
